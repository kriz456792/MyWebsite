About Me

When I was a child, my dream was to become an inventor. One time I decided to cut an electric cord, get a light bulb socket, a plug, and wired everything up. Fearfully I slowly plugged in my made-up lamp and to my surprise it worked, but when I tried unplugging it, I got shocked but that did not stop me. The very next week, I had a school project where I had to create a made-up cardboard electric device. I ended up making a small hole in the front of the made-up cardboard radio and tapping up my lamp through the hole, and I also didn't forget to rewire the made-up lamp so that it wouldn't shock me again. The day I presented my made-up cardboard radio I felt proud.

It would be a long time before I would try to pursue technology. First, at the age of 18(close to 19), I decided to pursue entrepreneurship. I started a small business selling "As seen on TV" products all over California, in some parts of Arizona and in Nevada. My business generated between 250,000 and 500,000 yearly with a few employees and myself. In this chapter of my life, I learned a lot about running a business, managing people, and most importantly I learned about failure.

Fast Forward a couple of years, and I decided to pursue a college education. I didn't know what field of study I'd pursue, but I knew I'd never give up. Eventually I would gain interest in Software Development, and I would make the decision to pursue a career in technology. Today is very clear that the child in me who dreamed about becoming an inventor now can make an impact, to be part of something great, to develop software, systems, applications. All things that other people and businesses could use.

On a weekly basis I spend a large chunk of my time programming, working out, reading, and listening to podcast.
